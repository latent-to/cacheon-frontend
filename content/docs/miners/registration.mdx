## Everything before commit needs no chain

You can write, scan, verify, GPU-evaluate, and bench a bundle without a wallet and without touching any chain. See [Local testing](/docs/miners/local-testing). The chain matters only when you submit. To submit and be paid you need a Bittensor **hotkey**, which is standard Bittensor wallet tooling on netuid 14 (mainnet) or 470 (testnet).

## Commit-reveal

Submission uses **commit-reveal** so nobody can copy your bundle off-chain and front-run you. You post a hash of your bundle first (the commit), then reveal the bundle later. Revealing proves you committed that exact content first.

```bash
# 1) commit a hash of your bundle (during the commit window)
optima commit my_bundle --hotkey <YOUR_HOTKEY> --salt <random> --round N

# 2) reveal it (during the reveal window) — proves you committed it first
optima reveal my_bundle --hotkey <YOUR_HOTKEY> --salt <random> --round N
```

Keep your `--salt` and your bundle. You need both to reveal, and the salt is what binds the hash to you.

<Callout type="warn" title="A matched earlier commit earns 0">
  A reveal whose content matches an *earlier* commit by a **different** hotkey is flagged a **copy** and
  earns 0. The match is not just an exact hash: a reformat-invariant fingerprint catches a bundle that
  was re-whitespaced or had its symbols renamed, and a per-slot, per-file check catches a stolen kernel
  body that was relocated into an imported module or hidden by padding the bundle with an extra op. Copy
  detection is cumulative across rounds.
</Callout>

## Settlement

Settlement is run by the validator. It scores every **original, passing** reveal and applies **king-of-the-hill**: the best kernel for a slot is the **champion**, and to take the title a challenger must beat it by the margin (`your_score ≥ champion_score × (1 + 2%)`). A bit-identical copy ties, never clears the margin, and earns 0.

```bash
# per-slot championships: one champion per slot, emission split across slots
optima settle --per-slot --round N

# inspect ledger state (champion, counts)
optima ledger
```

There is one champion per slot and emission is split across slots that have a champion, so you can win by **specializing** in a single slot. You do not need to beat everyone everywhere.

<Callout type="info" title="The chain is a local JSON ledger today">
  Commit, reveal, and settle currently operate on a local JSON ledger (`optima_ledger.json`), not a live
  chain. Full chain integration is on the roadmap. The hotkey identity and copy-detection logic are real;
  the on-chain plumbing is not wired up yet.
</Callout>
