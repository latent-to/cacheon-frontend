Cacheon is built by a small team with backgrounds in quantitative research, Bittensor subnet design, and large-scale infrastructure engineering.

## Xavier Lyu -- Research

[Twitter](https://x.com/xavi3rlu) · [GitHub](https://github.com/xavierlyu) · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/xavier-lyu)

Xavier started at **Pantera Capital** as a quant developer, building data pipelines and ML infrastructure. He then moved into fundamental investment analysis under a seasoned PM: financial modeling, TAM & PMF evaluation, how a company generates revenue, sitting on management calls. This combination means he thinks about Cacheon the same way: does the mechanism work technically, and does the business model actually make sense.

At the **Opentensor Foundation**, Xavier worked under Jacob Steeves reviewing subnet proposals, analyzing incentive mechanisms, and identifying potential miner exploits. He also co-designed **dTAO tokenomics**, running the simulations and analysis that informed how token flow works across the network.

## Clément Blaise -- Infrastructure

[GitHub](https://github.com/clementblaise) · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/clément-blaise-17ba35125)

At **ConsenSys**, Clément built an internal developer platform from scratch, defined its technical direction, and grew the team around it. Before that, he ran SRE on large-scale **Kubernetes** platforms at **Crédit Agricole**, one of the largest banks in Europe. He has always worked close to the metal with open-source tooling and has a habit of tracing problems to the root rather than the nearest visible layer.
