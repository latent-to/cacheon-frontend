Optima is built by a small team with backgrounds in quantitative research, Bittensor subnet design, and large-scale infrastructure engineering.

## Xavier Lyu — Research

[Twitter](https://x.com/xavi3rlu) · [GitHub](https://github.com/xavierlyu) · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/xavier-lyu)

Xavier started at **Pantera Capital** as a quant developer, building data pipelines and ML infrastructure. He then moved into a fundamental investment analyst role doing financial modeling and due diligence on protocol economics, market sizing, competitive dynamics, etc.

At the **Opentensor Foundation**, Xavier worked under Jacob Steeves reviewing subnets, analyzing incentive mechanisms, and identifying potential exploits. He also co-designed **dTAO tokenomics** by running simulations.

## Clément Blaise — Infrastructure

[GitHub](https://github.com/clementblaise) · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/clément-blaise-17ba35125)

At **ConsenSys**, Clément built an internal developer platform from scratch, defined its technical direction, and grew the team around it. Before that, he ran SRE on large-scale Kubernetes platforms at **Crédit Agricole**, one of the largest banks in Europe. He has always worked close to the metal with open-source tooling and has a habit of tracing problems to the root rather than the nearest visible layer.

## Dera Okeke — Documentation

[GitHub](https://github.com/chideraao) · [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/dera-okeke)

Dera is an experienced documentation engineer with a passion for making complex systems clear and usable. He focuses on developer-facing infrastructure, turning complex concepts into practical, well-structured guides.

He currently works on the official Bittensor documentation, helping shape how the network and its tooling are understood by developers.
