title: MiniMax-M3 case study
---

The MiniMax-M3 work established that meaningful serving improvements can come
from bounded collective epilogues rather than from replacing an entire model
runtime. It also exposed the evidence gaps that led to the current hardened
referee.

This is historical engineering evidence, not a current-path crown record.

## Workload and target

The campaign used MiniMax-M3-NVFP4 at tensor parallelism four on B300-class
hardware. The strongest proposals fused parts of the MoE tail where expert
output, reduction, residual addition, and RMS normalization otherwise cross
several launch and communication boundaries.

That work informed the current registered identities:

- `collective.ar_residual_rmsnorm`;
- `collective.moe_finalize_ar_rmsnorm`; and
- atomic `collective.moe_epilogue.v1`, which owns and displaces both singleton
  epilogue identities when they are one inseparable change.

The atomic target matters economically: a single fused implementation should
not earn duplicate permanent titles for two overlapping views of the same
delta.

## Historical measurements

Under the pre-hardening harness, the shallow fused path measured `1.044×` and
`1.049×` in two runs. The deeper fused path measured `1.074×` and `1.071×`.
A later chain-loop reproduction measured `1.072×` for the deep bundle. The
recorded audit samples reported zero violations in those campaigns.

Those results are useful evidence that the optimization surface is real. They
do **not** prove a crown under today's architecture because they predate the
complete current controls: injected arena services, exact target resolution,
isolated whole-engine B/C/B′ sessions, pristine T authority, retained
exact-schema evidence, transactional two-pass settlement, and the separate
integrated release stack.

## What the campaign taught us

### Optimize the whole owned boundary

A reduce-only slot cannot express overlap with its producer. Giving
`moe.fused_experts_reduce` ownership of the trailing reduction and defining a
bounded fused epilogue target created semantic units large enough for real
communication/launch wins while keeping a validator-owned output contract.

### Direct rollout comparison can be misleading

Large low-bit MoE launches showed that two nominally identical engines could
produce noisy logit differences. That made self-reported or direct rollout KL
an unreliable universal authority. The current design seals externally
observed B/C/B′ trajectories and grades them later with a separate candidate-free
pristine T engine.

### Graph behavior is part of correctness

An eager-only correct kernel can disappear under graph capture or behave
differently on replay. The current verifier captures applicable entries,
refreshes dynamic inputs, poisons outputs, and checks multiple replays; the
production referee then observes the complete graph-enabled Engine.

### Candidate code cannot share the controller boundary

Native extensions and Python hooks were once too close to trusted timing and
quality logic. The hardened design builds in disposable prebuild workers,
executes each complete arm in a no-egress OCI lifetime, times from the host, and
destroys C before pristine T receives sealed trajectories.

### Reproduction is a state, not a note

Two promising measurements written in a worklog are not equivalent to two
independent retained qualification attempts. The current settlement state
machine records a first PASS as `reproduction_pending`, reopens both attempts,
and uses the lower matching speedup.

## Current interpretation

The historical B300 numbers should be read as discovery and design evidence.
The current hardened path has structural synthetic settlement and dry-run
weight evidence, but no current-path real B300 crown or final production Engine
image served end to end. Those are explicit remaining gates, not implied by
the older campaign.
